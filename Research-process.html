Research process

The initial stages of the process involved story circles in which the participants shared stories and objects representing an aspect of their lived experiences as an academic of working-class heritage (WCH). The story circles were facilitated to provide the participants with opportunities to share, unsettle, interrogate and (re)interpret stories that represented their being and becoming academics of WCH.   
 
After the story circles, the group broke up into three smaller groups to analyse narrative data from the story circles to create composite stories on the following three themes: 

  • What it means to be an academic of working-class heritage
  • Career routes into and through academia as an academic of working-class heritage
  • Developing epistemic confidence as an academic of working-class heritage

Composite stories involve identifying themes across different stories and weaving them into a coherent narrative.  
 
Once the composite stories were identified and drafted, an illustrator worked with the participants to create the stories in an interactive comic format. These stories contained illustrations with embedded multimedia content in the form of audio extracts from the story circles and links to resources such as videos and journal articles.  
 
The dissemination stage of the process involved hosting the stories on this website and asking people who engage with them to respond to them by sharing their own stories of being and becoming a person of WCH in academia.
