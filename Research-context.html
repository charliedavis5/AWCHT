Research context

This project created a space for academics identifying as being of working-class heritage to share stories about their routes into academia. The storytelling process led to the creation of three stories in an interactive comic format communicating and celebrating the rich complexities of experience associated with identifying as an academic of working-class experience.  
 
We suggest that that the final stories can provide working-class people in, or considering transition into higher education spaces, with representations that people like them exist in the Academy. 

Story Circle 3 - Emotional work on the study

The lived experiences of academics of working-class heritage (WCH) continue to be of interest to researchers from multiple disciplinary fields. In many instances, academics of WCH are likely to be the objects of research about their lives as opposed to being the inquirers (Walkerdine, 2021). Representations of academics of WCH are often couched in stereotypes of the plucky socially mobile hero escaping the hopelessness of working-class life in pursuit of an idyllic middle-class existence or the perpetual down-trodden victim. 

Such stories limit the possibilities of expression for academics who experience the rich complexities of being and becoming an academic of WCH. Mobilising a participatory research appraoch, this study used critical storytelling methods to articulate the rich cultural wealth of being and becoming an academic of WCH. The study sought to create an inquiry space were through the co-production of knowledge the participants could generate critical hope needed to challenge pernicious stereotypes representing the lives of academics of WCH in a limited range of deficit tropes.
